# Creative Idea Matrix SOP
## Generate Unlimited Ad Variations Using the SPINE Framework

---

## **THE FRAMEWORK**

```
SPINE (The Concept):
├─ Product
├─ Job to be Done / Result
├─ Core Problem
└─ Hero Mechanism

IF ANY OF THESE 4 CHANGE → NEW CONCEPT

↓

Within the same SPINE:

TAMs (3-7 personas)
×
Desire / Why / Motivation
×
UMOP / Manifestation / Symptom
×
Offer Wrap

= UNLIMITED VARIATIONS
```

---

## **STEP 1: BUILD THE SPINE**

The SPINE is your concept. If ANY of these 4 elements change, you have a NEW concept.

### **1. Product**
What you're selling

**Example:**
```
Beer Belly Buster
```

---

### **2. Job To Be Done / Result**
What the customer wants to accomplish

**Example:**
```
Lose the gut / Look attractive / Feel healthy
```

---

### **3. Core Problem**
What's blocking them from the result. ONE problem only. If you have multiple problems, you have multiple concepts.

**Example:**
```
Beer belly won't go away despite diet and exercise
```

---

### **4. Hero Mechanism**
HOW your product uniquely solves the core problem. This is the core — it never changes.

**Example:**
```
Targets visceral fat through hormonal regulation 
(cortisol + insulin), not just calorie deficit
```

---

**✅ SPINE Complete = You have THE CONCEPT**

---

## **STEP 2: IDENTIFY TAMs**

List 3-7 different personas who share the same SPINE but are different people.

TAMs do NOT change the concept. They are different humans experiencing the same problem.

**Example:**
```
TAM 1: Dads (35-50, has young kids)
TAM 2: Lifters (gym-goers, lift regularly)
TAM 3: Bachelors (single men, dating-focused)
TAM 4: Professionals (desk job, health-conscious)
TAM 5: Ex-Athletes (used to be fit)
```

---

## **STEP 3: CREATIVE LANGUAGE**

For each TAM, define three things. This is where the concept gets translated into their world.

---

### **A) Desire / Why / Motivation**

What does this TAM actually want? Not what they need — what they're lying awake thinking about. This sets the emotional tone and gives the ad its energy.

**Example:**
```
TAM 1: DADS
Desire: Be the dad who shows up — energy to play, present for every moment

TAM 2: LIFTERS
Desire: Look like they lift. Get the respect their work in the gym deserves.

TAM 3: BACHELORS
Desire: Confidence. Be the guy in the room who doesn't think twice about taking his shirt off.

TAM 4: PROFESSIONALS
Desire: Look as successful as they are. Health and longevity to enjoy what they've built.

TAM 5: EX-ATHLETES
Desire: Get back to who they were. Prove they still have it.
```

---

### **B) UMOP / Manifestation / Symptom**

HOW does the problem show up for this TAM? What is the specific moment they feel it?

**This is the hook. This is where you stop the scroll.**

A UMOP can be one of three things — and you should aim for a mix of all three per TAM:

- **Unique Mechanism of the Problem** — explains WHY the problem exists in a way that reframes why their previous attempts failed (e.g. "most courts skip the shock-absorbing layer because it's too expensive — that's why your knees hurt even on a new court")
- **Manifestation** — a specific observable moment in their life (e.g. "wife keeps poking your beer belly")
- **Symptom** — a physical or practical consequence they experience (e.g. "can't put your socks on in the morning")

All three are the SAME problem. Just experienced and expressed differently.

**Example:**
```
TAM 1: DADS

UMOP 1 [Symptom]: Getting winded chasing your kids around the yard
UMOP 2 [Manifestation]: Wife keeps making comments about your gut
UMOP 3 [Symptom]: Can't put your socks on without sitting down
UMOP 4 [Manifestation]: Embarrassed to take your shirt off at the pool

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

TAM 2: LIFTERS

UMOP 1 [Mechanism]: Lifting 4x a week but visceral fat doesn't respond to training — it's hormonal, not caloric
UMOP 2 [Manifestation]: People don't believe you lift when you have a shirt on
UMOP 3 [Manifestation]: Girlfriend keeps commenting on your gut
UMOP 4 [Symptom]: Before/after gym photos are depressing — nothing's changed

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

TAM 3: BACHELORS

UMOP 1 [Symptom]: Nervous to take your shirt off on dates
UMOP 2 [Symptom]: Dating app matches declining
UMOP 3 [Manifestation]: Can't compete with the fit guys in the room
UMOP 4 [Manifestation]: Avoiding beach and pool dates entirely

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

TAM 4: PROFESSIONALS

UMOP 1 [Symptom]: Doctor just flagged elevated blood pressure
UMOP 2 [Symptom]: Looking 10 years older than you are
UMOP 3 [Manifestation]: Can't button your suit jacket anymore
UMOP 4 [Symptom]: 2pm energy crash killing your productivity

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

TAM 5: EX-ATHLETES

UMOP 1 [Symptom]: Can't do what you did 5 years ago
UMOP 2 [Manifestation]: Old teammates don't recognise you
UMOP 3 [Manifestation]: Old photos are depressing to look at
UMOP 4 [Symptom]: Getting destroyed in pickup games you used to dominate
```

### **C) Offer Wrap**

This is the final piece. It takes the Hero Mechanism and dresses it in this TAM's language as the irresistible offer. It tells each specific TAM exactly how you're going to solve their version of this problem.

The Offer Wrap = Hero Mechanism + Offer, expressed in TAM language.

The core Hero Mechanism never changes. The expression of it does.

**Example:**
```
TAM 1: DADS
Offer Wrap: "30-day hormonal reset built for busy dads — 
get your energy back or we refund every cent."

TAM 2: LIFTERS
Offer Wrap: "30-day protocol that targets the visceral fat 
your training can't touch — visible results or full refund."

TAM 3: BACHELORS
Offer Wrap: "Lose the gut in 30 days without starving yourself — 
or your money back. 50% off this week only."

TAM 4: PROFESSIONALS
Offer Wrap: "Improve your health markers in 30 days — 
cortisol regulation, not calorie restriction. Refund if not."

TAM 5: EX-ATHLETES
Offer Wrap: "The 30-day protocol that targets what training 
can't fix — get your body back or we refund you."
```

## **THAT'S IT.**

Simple. Clean. No overthinking.

- **SPINE** = The concept. Locked forever. One product, one problem, one mechanism.
- **TAMs** = Different people with the same problem
- **Desire** = What they actually want — sets the emotional tone
- **UMOPs** = How the problem shows up in their life — the hook
- **Offer Wrap** = The hero mechanism expressed in their language — closes the ad

Mix and match = unlimited variations. Same concept. Different doors in.
