# Headline Writer

A specialized skill for crafting high-converting headlines grounded in conversion data and proven copywriting principles.

## When to Use

- Writing or rewriting headlines for landing pages, ads, or emails
- Critiquing an existing headline for clarity or conversion potential
- Generating headline variations for A/B testing
- Matching headline style to traffic temperature (cold, warm, retargeting)

## Core Principles

**The headline is 80 cents of the dollar** (Ogilvy). Five times more people read the headline than body copy. A weak headline renders everything else irrelevant.

### Length Rules
- **Headlines**: 6–12 words. Shorter wins. Straightforward beats clever 88% of the time (15.4% CTR vs 7.1% — Sumo, 150K headlines).
- **Subheadlines**: 15–25 words. Expand the headline promise and bridge into paragraph one.

### Traffic Temperature Rule (overrides everything)
- **Cold traffic** (Meta ads, cold outreach): Benefit or curiosity headline — never a brand name.
- **Warm traffic** (retargeting, email list): Direct offer headline is acceptable.

## Workflow

1. **Identify traffic temperature** — ask if not already known.
2. **Clarify the offer** — specific result, niche, timeframe, differentiator.
3. **Select formula** — choose based on traffic temperature and offer type.
4. **Produce 3–5 headline variations** using `references/headline-formulas.md`.
5. **Apply the 3-second test** — would someone unfamiliar with the business immediately understand: what you do, who it's for, and why they should care?
6. **Flag variants for A/B testing** — always recommend testing at least 2 variants.

## 3-Second Test

Before finalising any headline, verify it passes: could a stranger read it, cover it, and accurately explain the offer? If not — rewrite.
