# Subheadline Patterns Reference

Patterns, templates, and critique tools for writing high-converting subheadlines.

---

## Core Formula

```
Who it's for + How it works + Proof or Risk Reversal
```

The sub must answer every question the headline raises:
- **Who is this for?**
- **How does it work?**
- **What if it doesn't work?**

---

## Pattern 1: Qualification + Mechanism + Guarantee

**Structure:** `For [specific audience qualifier] — [mechanism/delivery method], [risk reversal].`

**Example:**
> "For supplement and eCom brands doing $50K+/month — fully done-for-you, month-to-month, with a performance guarantee or we work free."

**Fill-in-the-blank:**
`For [niche] doing [revenue qualifier] — [delivery method], [guarantee or risk reversal].`

---

## Pattern 2: Without Stack

**Structure:** `Without [objection 1], without [objection 2], and without [objection 3].`

**When to use:** When the headline makes a bold promise that raises multiple "but what about…" objections.

**Example:**
> "Without extra ad spend, without in-house staff, and without touching your existing tech stack."

**Note:** Best used as the second half of a compound sub — lead with the "who it's for" qualifier, then add the Without stack.

---

## Pattern 3: Specificity Bridge

**Structure:** `[Expand the headline's number/claim] by [mechanism], in [timeframe], [qualifying detail].`

**When to use:** When the headline states a result and the reader needs to know *how*.

**Example (paired with a revenue headline):**
> "We build and manage your complete Klaviyo ecosystem — flows, campaigns, segmentation — and guarantee measurable revenue growth within 30 days."

**Fill-in-the-blank:**
`We [verb] your [thing] — [specific components] — and [guarantee or outcome] within [timeframe].`

---

## Pattern 4: Reader Identification + Stakes

**Structure:** `If you're a [specific person] who [situation], [consequence/what's at stake].`

**When to use:** When strong reader qualification is needed and the audience is broad.

**Example:**
> "If you're an eCom or supplement brand doing $50K–$5M/month, your email list is likely leaving 30–40% of your revenue on the table."

## Headline/Subheadline Pairs — Worked Examples

### Cold Traffic (Meta/paid)

| Headline | Subheadline |
|---|---|
| We Help Supplement Brands Generate $100K+ in Monthly Email Revenue in 90 Days | For brands doing $50K+/month — done-for-you Klaviyo management with a performance guarantee or we work free. |
| How One Supplement Brand Added $214K in Email Revenue in 60 Days | See the exact flows, segmentation strategy, and send cadence we used — and how we can replicate it for your brand. |

### Warm Traffic (retargeting, email list)

| Headline | Subheadline |
|---|---|
| The Email Team Behind 7 and 8-Figure Supplement Brands | Month-to-month engagements. Full Klaviyo ownership. Measurable revenue growth — or we work free until we deliver. |
| Ready to Stop Leaving 30–40% of Your Revenue on the Table? | We audit your flows, build what's missing, and manage every send — fully done-for-you. |

## Critique Checklist

Run every draft subheadline through this list before finalising:

- [ ] **15–30 words?** Count the words. Trim or split if over.
- [ ] **Prospect-facing?** Remove any language that's "about us" rather than about what they get.
- [ ] **Qualifies the reader?** Does it tell the right person "this is for you"?
- [ ] **Expands the headline?** Does it add specificity the headline didn't include?
- [ ] **Handles the first objection?** What will they doubt after reading the headline — and does the sub address it?
- [ ] **No filler?** "E2 Agency is a full-service firm…" type descriptions = rewrite.
- [ ] **One breath test?** Read it aloud. If you run out of air — cut it.

## Common Rewrites

### Before (company-description mode)
> "E2 Agency is a full-service email marketing firm specialising in Klaviyo for eCom and supplement brands."

### After (prospect-facing, benefit-driven)
> "For supplement and eCom brands doing $50K+/month — fully done-for-you Klaviyo management with a performance guarantee or we work free."
