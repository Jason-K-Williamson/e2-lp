# Subheadline Writer

A focused skill for crafting subheadlines that convert curiosity into commitment.

## When to Use

- Writing or rewriting a subheadline to pair with an existing headline
- Critiquing a subheadline for reader-centricity, specificity, or length
- Ensuring the subheadline qualifies the right reader and neutralises their first objection
- Building headline/subheadline pairs for landing pages or B2B service pages

## Core Principle

**The headline earns the glance. The subheadline earns the scroll.**

If the headline creates "interesting…", the subheadline must convert that into "I need to keep reading." Its only job is to keep them reading.

## The Three Jobs of a Great Subheadline

1. **Expand the promise** — add specificity or mechanism to the headline's claim
   - Headline: *"We Turn Your Email List Into a Revenue Engine"*
   - Sub: *"Without Extra Ad Spend, Without In-House Staff, and Without Touching Your Existing Tech Stack."*

2. **Qualify the reader** — make it clear who this is specifically for, so the right person leans in and the wrong person self-selects out
   - *"For eCom and supplement brands doing $50K–$5M/month who know email should be generating more."*

3. **Handle the first objection** — whatever doubt the headline creates, the sub neutralises it
   - Bold claim headline → sub adds the credibility layer ("here's why it's real")

## Length Rule

**15–30 words.** Long enough to do the job. Short enough to read in one breath. If the subheadline needs two commas and two "and"s to get through — it's doing too much. Split it or cut it.

## The B2B Service Page Formula

```
Headline:     The big promise — specific and direct
Subheadline:  Who it's for + how it works + proof or risk reversal
```

**E2 Agency example:**
- Headline: *"We Add $50K–$200K in Annual Email Revenue to eCom Brands in 90 Days"*
- Sub: *"For supplement and eCom brands doing $50K+/month — fully done-for-you, month-to-month, with a performance guarantee or we work free."*

The sub answers every immediate question the headline raises: Who is this for? How does it work? What if it doesn't?

## The #1 Mistake to Avoid

Writing the subheadline as a **description of the company** rather than a **continuation of the promise to the reader.**

> ❌ "E2 Agency is a full-service email marketing firm specialising in Klaviyo."
> ✅ "For supplement brands doing $50K+/month — done-for-you email with a performance guarantee."

Nobody cares about you in the first 3 seconds. They care about what they get. Keep it prospect-facing, benefit-driven, and specific — always.

## Workflow

1. **Read the headline** — understand the promise already made.
2. **Identify the first objection** — what will the reader doubt or question?
3. **Clarify who it's for** — specific niche, revenue range, or situation.
4. **Write the subheadline** using the formula: *Who it's for + how it works + proof/risk reversal*.
5. **Check length** — trim to 15–30 words.
6. **Check perspective** — is every word prospect-facing? Rewrite any "about us" language.
